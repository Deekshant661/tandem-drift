feat: recovery system, arcade handling tuning, further engine audio polish

Recovery (press R): server-authoritative respawn at the last passed
checkpoint (or spawn), facing the correct direction, at rest. New
Checkpoint.angle field (derived from road tangent / gate chaining) makes
respawn orientation correct on every map. A dedicated recovered message
(separate from the 20Hz snapshot cadence) lets the client time a black
fade-out/hold/fade-in transition precisely instead of guessing against
latency; Predictor.hardReset and SnapshotBuffer.reset ensure no residual
smoothing glides the car back from its pre-recovery position. A stuck-
detection prompt (Press R to Recover) appears after ~4s of near-zero
speed. A server-side out-of-bounds safety net auto-recovers the car if it
ever ends up implausibly far from the playable world (a defensive check,
not a normal occurrence -- the closed road walls already prevent it).
Recovery is rate-limited server-side and ignored from spectators.

Note on the auto-right after a flip ask: this sim is intentionally flat
(Phase 2 design decision) -- there is no rollover physics for a car to
flip out of, so implementing a fake visual flip-and-correct would not
correspond to any real state. The stuck-detection prompt covers the same
practical need (wedged against scenery) within this architecture.

Driving feel: steering authority now ramps in earlier (full at 4 m/s) and
gently tapers above 20 m/s for high-speed stability instead of staying
razor-twitchy at any speed; snappier drive force, softer brake force,
more controllable (less icy) handbrake drift. Keyboard input is now ramped
(steer ~140ms, throttle ~200ms to full) instead of an instant on/off
switch, which is most of what makes keyboard steering feel analog.

Engine audio: added a master compressor across every sound as a clipping/
harshness safety net, and further mellowed the high-speed band (lower
filter cutoff, narrower pitch range) so top speed sounds throaty rather
than harsh.

flow, ramp math, checkpoint angle correctness via existing map tests,
Predictor.hardReset, SnapshotBuffer.reset, GameClient recovery wiring).
